This is a league based challenge.

For this challenge, multiple versions of the same game are available. Once you have proven your skills on this first version, you will access a higher league and extra rules will be unlocked.

  The Goal

To win the match, you will have to manage your troops to defeat all the enemy soldiers.

  Rules

This is a 1 vs 1 game, when both players have the same amount of units and they have to use strategy to defeat the other player destroying all his/her units.


In each turn, the player could send one troop to the position that his/her wants, to do this you will have to indicate the unit id, how many points it would move in the x axis and how many points it would move in the y axis. 


All the units have four states (adding one more to the archers), they are the idle position (when they aren’t doing anything), moving, fighting and death (when the unit has been destroyed), the archer have the fire position, when they are attacking to other units with their bows.


The units have a direction too, this is what direction are they looking at, this are NORTHWEST (0), NORTH (1), NORTHEAST (2), EAST (3), SOUTHEAST (4), SOUTH (5), SOUTHWEST (6) and WEST (7).


There are four types of units, these are soldiers with swords, spearmans, archers and cavalry. This follows a “Rock paper scissors” scheme, where the sword beats the spear, the spear beats the horse and the horse beats the sword. The archer can attack at units that are at a distance less than 200 points from their position, but are very weak at hand-to-hand combat.


The game is divided into three leagues, adding complexity when the leagues advance, each leagues has more units in each side that the last one. The first league will have four units: a swordsman, a spearman, an archer and a cavalry; the second one will have nine units, which uses a draft system, where each player will select one unit each turn until there are nine units on each side of the map (how it works it will be explained later); and the third one will have thirty units on each side with a draft system, but it adds the general units, this units are a bit stronger than the other ones and can change the ally units stats depending of his status and position (it will be explained later).


The draft is a troops selector, where you can choose between the four types of troops and you can indicate where you want to put your different type of units selecting their initial x and y position (for example “2(id) 200(x) 500(y)”). The selector is:


1. Soldiers with swords.

2. Soldiers with spears.

3. Cavalry.

4. Archers.


The general is a special unit that (in the third league) always would be the unit that has id 1 in your army. This unit have an amount of 1.25 in his stats permanently and increment all the ally units stats that are near to him, but if the general dies, all your units would receive a stats penalization that would decrease their stats in 0.75. All the stats can change, except for the life one, that it isn’t affected by these changes.


There are different types of stats, and each unit has different amount of them, the stats are: life, defense, attack, charge resistance, charge force, speed and resistance from far attacks, in addition, the archers have two more stats, these ones are how far they can throw arrows and how damage they do with these arrows.


The game finish when one army is completely destroy or when 400 turns finish, in these last case, the winner would be the player who has more units alive at that moment, being a draw if both players have the same amount of units.

  Game Input

Input for one game turn
First input: 1 integer numberUnits (how many troops are on each side at the beginning).
Second input: 1 integer allyUnitId.
Third input: 1 integer allyUnitPosX.
Fourth input: 1 integer allyUnitPosY.
Fifth input: 1 integer allyUnitDirection.
Sixth input: 1 integer allyUnitLife.
Seventh input: 1 integer allyUnitType.
Eight input: 1 integer enemyUnitId.
Nineth input: 1 integer enemyUnitPosX.
Tenth input: 1 integer enemyUnitPosY.
Eleventh input: 1 integer enemyUnitDirection.
Twelfth input: 1 integer enemyUnitLife.
Thirteenth input: 1 integer enemyUnitType.
Output for one game turn
Line 1: 3 space separated integers id, movement in X axis and movement in Y axis.

You can send more than one order (without limit) in one turn separating them with ";", for example "2 0 300 ; 4 100 0".

If you don't want to move any troops, the first integer should be 0, for example "0 0 0" or "0 200 50".
